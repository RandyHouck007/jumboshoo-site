Network, Power and Response

The system must work where there is no grid, no towers, and harsh conditions. Jumbo Shoo is engineered for ultra-low power, long-range telemetry, and fast, gentle response.

LoRa in the bush

  • Long-range, low-power LoRa radio links sensor nodes to a local brain without any SIM cards or external infrastructure.
  • Radio parameters are tuned to balance range, latency, and battery life within regional regulations.
  • Critical events are tiny packets, carefully engineered so the alarm arrives before the elephant does.

Off-grid power

  • Solar-charged batteries power nodes sized for multi-day autonomy under poor weather.
  • Passive geophones and aggressive duty-cycling keep standby power extremely low.

The protection ladder

  • Green – Observe: Quietly track movement and learn corridors.
  • Yellow – Warn: Send SMS and voice alerts to farmers and rangers, trigger road signs.
  • Orange – Gently deter: Activate targeted lights, infrasound thumps, bee or predator sounds.
  • Red – Human response: Dispatch trained teams with non-lethal tools only when necessary.

The same rigor used in advanced defense systems is repurposed here for targeting protection, not harm.
