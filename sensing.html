Sensing Like an Elephant

Jumbo Shoo builds a "nervous system for the landscape" by combining seismic, acoustic, optical, thermal, and satellite-derived intelligence into one coherent picture.

Layered sensing

  • Seismic: Geophones pick up low-frequency footfalls that travel through soil, even at night and through brush.
  • Acoustic: Microphones capture rumbles and movement, helping distinguish elephants from people, vehicles, or livestock.
  • Optical and thermal: Low-power cameras and thermal imagers provide visual confirmation at key choke points.
  • Collars and drones: GPS collars and tactical drones close the loop for proactive monitoring and rapid checks.

From raw signals to decisions

  • Edge devices filter and compress signals at the node, extracting features such as gait rhythm, coherence, and direction of travel.
  • Multiple nodes vote together, reducing false alarms from wind, vehicles, or livestock.
  • Only high-value, highly confident events travel over the radio link, preserving battery life and radio airtime.

The result is a system that feels the landscape the way elephants do, but reacts quickly enough to protect people and crops.
